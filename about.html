Wall Street Plaza

Wall Street Plaza is a small plaza located on the Lower East Side of Manhattan in New York City. The plaza has a small waterfall tours the back of the plaza, adjacent front Street, opposite Water Street.

Is not an official 911 Memorial place, but it does hold a special meaning for some of us who witnessed, and lived through 911. It has a nice breeze and comfortable benches, for you to enjoy some downtime, while eating lunch and visiting New York City. There are plenty of places to shop around the area. Century 21 department store, is just a few blocks away from this Plaza, located at 1972 Broadway.

Century 21 Department Store

For those of you who are interested in visiting this Plaza, you can travel there by bus or train.

Directions:

  • You can take the J train to Broad Street/Wall Street station and then walk down tours Water Street.

  • The 4, 5 trains stop at Wall Street station, you walk from there tours Water Street.

  • You can also take the 2,3,4,5 trains to Fulton Street, and then walk from there tours water Street.

You can view and read information about Wall Street Plaza and the Staten Island 911 Memorial. If you are interested in more information about 911, you can visit the official 911 website: Home or visit the official National September 11 Memorial and Museum, located in downtown Manhattan, at 180 Greenwich St, New York, NY 10007.

Memorial: Open Daily 7:30 a.m. to 9 p.m.

Museum: Open Daily Sunday to Thursday 9 a.m. to 8 p.m. (6 p.m. last entry) Friday and Saturday 9 a.m. to 9 p.m. (7 p.m. last entry)

Staten Island 911 Memorial

What is special/meaningful about it? This memorial is dedicated to the 275 victims of the September 11th terrorist attack on New York City that lived on Staten Island

Why should other people visit it? It has a beautiful view of lower Manhattan from across the waters here in the north shore of Staten Island. During the anniversary of September 11th you have a unique view of the light towers projected form the World Trade Center site.

Best way to travel there?

Directions:

  • Take public transportation to the Staten Island Ferry (free ride for all commuters) and walk to the right following the water line.
